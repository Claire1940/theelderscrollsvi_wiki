<Section icon={Target} title="Deep RPG Mechanics and Character Progression">

A core component of any **the elder scrolls vi wishlist** is the demand for "crunchier" RPG mechanics. Many veterans felt that Skyrim oversimplified character development by removing attributes and classes. For the next entry, the community is calling for a return to traditional D&D-inspired systems that force players to make meaningful choices about their character's identity and limitations.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Return of Classes">
    - **Defined Roles:** Reintroduce classes to impact world reactivity and dialogue options.
    - **Skill Requirements:** Lock faction advancement behind relevant attribute levels.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Traits & Backgrounds">
    - **Meaningful Starts:** Use Starfield-style traits with both pros and cons.
    - **Origin Stories:** Backgrounds that change how NPCs perceive and interact with you.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Choice & Consequence">
    - **Branching Paths:** Quests that can fail or lead to vastly different world states.
    - **Reputation:** Joining one faction should realistically lock you out of rivals.
  </Card>

The goal is to move away from the "leader of every guild" trope. If you want to be the Arch-Mage, you should be required to actually master high-level magic, rather than just completing a linear questline with a sword in hand. This level of reactivity makes the world feel alive and rewards specialized builds.

| Feature | Skyrim System | ES6 Wishlist Proposal | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attributes** | Health/Magicka/Stamina only | Strength, Agility, Intelligence, etc. | Deeper build variety |
| **Factions** | Can join and lead all | Mutually exclusive factions | High replayability |
| **Reputation** | Minimal world reaction | Dynamic faction-based reputation | Realistic social world |
| **Leveling** | Skill-based only | Skill-based with Perk/Attribute caps | Balanced progression |

<Callout type="tip" title="Role-playing Tip">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Overhaul: Visceral and Meaty">

Combat in the Elder Scrolls series has often been criticized for feeling "floaty" or lacking impact. To compete with modern action RPGs, **the elder scrolls vi** needs a complete overhaul of its martial systems. Fans are looking for combat that feels visceral and reactive, drawing inspiration from titles like Vermintide or even the modding community's "MCO" (Modern Combat Overhaul) projects.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Movement Mechanics">
    Implement dedicated dodge, parry, and block systems that require timing and stamina management rather than just stat-checking.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Weapon Variety">
    Bring back spears, halberds, and throwing weapons. Each weapon type should have a unique move set and tactical advantage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Hybrid Magic">
    Adopt a system where you can cast spells while wielding two-handed weapons or shields, similar to the "Oblivion" style but refined.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Environmental Interaction">
    Allow magic and physical attacks to interact with the environment—freezing water, burning grass, or shattering stone covers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Weapon Type | Wishlist Feature | Expected Gameplay Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Spears** | Reach and Thrusting | Keep enemies at bay, vital for desert combat |
| **Curved Swords** | Speed and Bleed | Fast, aggressive playstyle for Redguard builds |
| **Magic Staffs** | Channeling and Utility | More than just "spell sticks"; used for defense |
| **Unarmed** | Dedicated Skill Tree | Viable monk-style builds with unique takedowns |

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Bethesda must avoid the "bullet sponge" difficulty scaling. Instead of just increasing enemy health, higher difficulty should improve enemy AI and introduce new combat maneuvers.
</Callout>

</Section>

For **the elder scrolls vi**, the wishlist includes a massive expansion of the Radiant AI system to support larger, denser populations without losing the "named NPC" charm.

Finally, the quality of writing is a major point on **the elder scrolls vi wishlist**. Fans are hoping for a shift toward more mature, nuanced storytelling that avoids the "chosen one" cliches. | Narrative Goal | Description | Inspiration |
