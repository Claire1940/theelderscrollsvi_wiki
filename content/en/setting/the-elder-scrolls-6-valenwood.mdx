The native Bosmer, also known as the Tree-Sap People, are a deeply misunderstood race. Unlike their cousins in Morrowind and Summerset, the Wood Elves are bound by the strict tenets of the **Green Pact**. This ancient covenant dictates that they cannot harm, harvest, or consume any vegetation native to Valenwood. Consequently, the Bosmer are strictly carnivorous, relying on imported timber for building and consuming exclusively meat—including, in extreme ritualistic circumstances, the bodies of fallen enemies.

<Callout type="success" title="The Wild Hunt Ritual">
The most terrifying aspect of Bosmer lore is the Wild Hunt. This ancient ritual transforms participants into a mindless, shifting horde of bloodthirsty monsters that consume everything in their path. Once the enemy is destroyed, the monsters turn on themselves in a self-destructive frenzy, making it a weapon of absolute last resort.
</Callout>

To understand the current state of the province, one must look at the historical events that shaped its borders. Valenwood has spent centuries caught in a tug-of-war between imperial expansion and elven isolationism. While community debates on the [Elder Scrolls Fandom forum](https://elderscrolls.fandom.com/f/p/3042182809096553554) highlight Hammerfell as another strong candidate, the historical depth of the southern forests remains unmatched.

Following the Oblivion Crisis, the political landscape shifted dramatically. The rise of the totalitarian Thalmor led to a swift coup in Valenwood, effectively integrating the province into the Third Aldmeri Dominion. The Empire, severely weakened by the Great War, was forced to abandon its southern allies. Today, the province simmer under a cold war, with native factions secretly preparing for a massive uprising to reclaim their sovereignty and restore the ancient Camoran Dynasty.

<Callout type="info" title="The Camoran Usurper's Legacy">
The devastation caused by Haymon Camoran in the third era left deep cultural scars. Many Bosmer remain highly skeptical of any leader attempting to unify the province through magical or Daedric means, preferring localized tribal rule over centralized authority.
